<p><ul><li>Structural Impingement</li><li>Functional Impingement</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Impingement syndrome can be categorized into two main types: structural impingement and functional impingement. Structural impingement refers to physical abnormalities in the joint or surrounding tissues that cause compression and friction during movement. Functional impingement, on the other hand, is related to improper movement patterns or muscle imbalances that lead to impingement symptoms. Both types can result in pain, limited range of motion, and decreased function in the affected joint, requiring different treatment approaches for management.</p></p>
