<p><p>Pressure bandages are used to apply controlled pressure to a wound or injury to reduce swelling and promote healing. Short stretch bandages are designed to provide high resistance to stretch, making them suitable for managing edema or venous insufficiency. Long stretch bandages have higher elasticity, ideal for providing consistent pressure and support for sprains or strains. Multi-layer compression systems combine different bandages to provide varying levels of pressure, suitable for managing complex wounds like venous ulcers. Each type serves a specific purpose in wound care management.</p></p>
