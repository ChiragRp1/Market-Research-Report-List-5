<p><p>Electrical Energy Storage (EES) systems come in various types based on their storage mechanisms: Physical, Electromagnetic, and Chemical. Physical storage systems store energy in physical components like capacitors or flywheels. Electromagnetic systems use magnetic fields to store energy, such as in superconducting magnetic energy storage. Chemical storage systems utilize chemical reactions to store and release energy, like in batteries. Each type of EES system has its unique advantages and applications, catering to different energy storage needs in various industries.</p></p>
