<p><ul><li>Air Dry</li><li>Freeze Dry</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Kale powder can be produced in two main ways - air drying and freeze drying. Air drying involves exposing the kale to warm air until it reaches a powdered consistency, while freeze drying involves freezing the kale and then removing the moisture through a vacuum process. Both methods preserve the nutritional content of the kale, but freeze drying is often considered superior as it retains more of the original color, flavor, and nutrients. Consumers can choose between air dry and freeze dry kale powder based on their preferences and needs.</p></p>
