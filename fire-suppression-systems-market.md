<p><p>Fire suppression systems are classified into four main types: chemical, gaseous, water, and foam. Chemical fire suppression systems work by releasing a chemical agent that interacts with the fire to extinguish it. Gaseous systems release gases such as CO2 or FM-200 to remove oxygen from the fire. Water systems use water to cool and extinguish the flames. Foam systems combine foam and water to smother the fire. Each type of system is designed to combat different types of fires effectively.</p></p>
