<p><p>Food encapsulation is a process where active ingredients are enclosed within a protective matrix to enhance stability and control release. There are three main types of food encapsulation: microencapsulation, which involves particles ranging from 1-1000 µm in size, nanoencapsulation, which uses particles smaller than 100 nm, and hybrid encapsulation, which combines different encapsulation techniques. Each type offers unique advantages in terms of protecting sensitive ingredients, controlling release, and improving bioavailability in food products.</p></p>
