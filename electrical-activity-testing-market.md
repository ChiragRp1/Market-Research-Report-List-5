<p><p>Electrical activity testing is a critical diagnostic tool used in healthcare. This testing includes EEG (electroencephalography) to monitor brain activity, MEG (magnetoencephalography) to measure magnetic fields produced by brain activity, and EMG (electromyography) to record electrical activity in muscles. EEG is commonly used to diagnose epilepsy and sleep disorders, while MEG helps in localizing brain functions. EMG is crucial in diagnosing neuromuscular disorders. These tests provide valuable insights into neurological and muscle health, aiding in treatment planning and monitoring progress.</p></p>
