<p><p>Single packaged HVAC systems are a type of heating, ventilation, and air conditioning system that combine all components into one unit. Air-cooled systems use outside air to cool the refrigerant, while water-cooled systems use water to remove heat from the refrigerant. Air-cooled systems are more common in residential and small commercial buildings due to their simplicity and cost-effectiveness. Water-cooled systems, on the other hand, are typically used in larger commercial buildings where space is limited and there is a need for more efficient cooling.</p></p>
