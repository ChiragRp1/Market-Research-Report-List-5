<p><p>Interview microphones come in various types to suit different recording needs. Lavalier microphones are small and discreet, typically clipped onto the speaker's clothing for hands-free operation. Shotgun microphones are highly directional, picking up sound in a narrow beam pattern and reducing background noise. Both types are popular in the interview market, with lavalier microphones preferred for their convenience and versatility, while shotgun microphones are favored for their ability to capture clear, focused audio in noisy environments.</p></p>
