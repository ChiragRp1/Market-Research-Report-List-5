<p><p>Composite CNG cylinders are categorized into two main types - Type III and Type IV. Type III cylinders have a metal liner with a composite overwrap, while Type IV cylinders consist of a fully non-metallic design. Type III cylinders are often preferred for their higher impact resistance and cost-effectiveness, while Type IV cylinders are known for their lighter weight and improved safety characteristics. Both types are widely used in the automotive industry for storing compressed natural gas (CNG) and are gaining popularity due to their efficiency and sustainability.</p></p>
