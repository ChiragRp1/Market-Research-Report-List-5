Wet snorkels are designed to allow water to enter the tube but include a purge valve for easy clearing. Semi-dry snorkels have splash guards at the top to prevent water from entering, while all-dry snorkels have a fully sealed top to keep water out completely. Each type caters to different preferences and diving conditions, offering varying levels of comfort and performance for divers.</p></p>
