<p><p>Gas-phase-grown carbon fiber is classified into two main types: PAN-based and asphalt-based. PAN-based carbon fiber is produced from polyacrylonitrile precursor, which results in high strength and stiffness. On the other hand, asphalt-based carbon fiber is derived from petroleum by-products, offering lower cost but lesser mechanical properties. Both types of carbon fibers are widely used in various industries such as aerospace, automotive, and sporting goods for their lightweight and high-performance characteristics.</p></p>
